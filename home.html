The most common and most reliable method of the past is to hack passwords is a brute force attack. In this method, we repeatedly try to guess the password of the victim at the speed of over ten thousand queries per second. Facebook does use captcha based protection so this method is now impractical in hacking.

A brute force attack is a type of high-speed repeat attack in which a computer with reasonable high-speed networking speeds sends out hundreds or thousands of queries per second to the Facebook servers. The machine sends out a string starting at specific characters and continues to increase with each query. Upon correct combination, the access is granted and one can access the facebook account. Such an attack is often not safe nor very reliable since it can be easily countered by monitoring the queries send per IP. However, we apply a system of rapidly changing IP by Tor Network to do so and although a bit slow, we can launch a brute force attack of facebook servers. Although now obsolete, we have successfully hacked many times using brute force in the past.
